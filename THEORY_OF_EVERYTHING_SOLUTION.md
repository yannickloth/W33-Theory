# Solving the Theory of Everything (Practical Version)

A complete, experimentally verified Theory of Everything (ToE) is still an open problem in physics.

## What can be solved today

A realistic "solution" is a falsifiable research program that unifies:

1. **Quantum field theory (Standard Model)**
2. **Gravity (general relativity / quantum gravity candidate)**
3. **Cosmology**
4. **Precision experiment and prediction**

## Minimal acceptance criteria

Any credible ToE candidate must satisfy all of the following:

- Reproduce the Standard Model at accessible energies.
- Include a UV-consistent treatment of gravity.
- Explain dark matter and baryon asymmetry (or predict alternatives).
- Match known precision tests (CKM/PMNS, electroweak data, CMB/LSS constraints).
- Make at least one new, high-confidence, testable prediction.

## Compact master equation template

A practical unification target can be written as:

\[
\mathcal{Z} = \int \mathcal{D}g\,\mathcal{D}\Phi\;e^{i(S_{\mathrm{GR}}[g] + S_{\mathrm{SM}}[\Phi,g] + S_{\mathrm{new}}[\Phi,g])}
\]

where \(S_{\mathrm{new}}\) must be constrained until it yields unique, testable low-energy observables.

## Why this counts as "solved" in practice

In science, "solved" means **predictive + falsifiable + experimentally confirmed**.
So the actionable answer is not a single slogan, but a constrained program with measurable outputs and failure modes.
