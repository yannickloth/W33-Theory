## Mapping M12 Orbits to Cubic Surface Geometry and E6 Invariants

### 1. Orbit Representatives and Geometric Configurations
- Each orbit under composed M12 generators corresponds to a unique codeword.
- These codewords are mapped to the 27 lines on the cubic surface (E6 fundamental).
- Orbits can be grouped to represent double-sixes, tritangent planes, and conics.

### 2. Double-Sixes and Tritangent Planes
- Double-sixes: pairs of 6 lines, each orbit group can be mapped to a double-six configuration.
- Tritangent planes: sets of 3 lines with special incidence, identified via adjacency relations among codewords.
- Incidence structure is encoded in the orbit adjacency and stabilizer analysis.

### 3. E6 Cubic Invariant Contribution
- For each orbit, compute the contribution to the E6 cubic invariant using codeword triples.
- The sum over all orbit representatives reconstructs the classical E6 cubic invariant.
- This mapping provides a direct algebraic-geometric bridge between group action and invariant structure.
