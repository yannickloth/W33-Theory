The Weyl group of E6 has order 51840 and is the automorphism group of the unique simple group of order 25920, which can be described as any of: PSU₄(2), PSΩ₆⁻(2), PSp₄(3) or PSΩ₅(3). This establishes a fundamental isomorphism: the simple group PSp(4,3) (closely related to the symplectic generalized quadrangle W(3,3)) sits inside the Weyl group W(E6) as a subgroup of index 2.

### Symplectic Geometry over F₃ and Configurations

The paper by Hoffman and Weintraub on symplectic geometry over F₃ is the most directly relevant source. In terms of the symplectic geometry of V, the maximal subgroups of index 27 and 36 are characterized by the concept of a spread of nonsingular pairs (nsp-spread) and the related concept of a double-six. There turn out to be 27 nsp-spreads and 36 double-sixes, and these are the configurations whose stabilizer subgroups are the subgroups of index 27 and 36 respectively.

### Double-Sixes and the Stabilizer Structure

The subgroup of G stabilizing a double-six is isomorphic to S₆, the symmetric group on 6 elements. The isomorphism is given by the action of the stabilizer on the doublets in a double-six. More specifically, since G acts transitively on the 36 double-sixes, the stabilizer H has order 25920/36 = 720.

### Connection to Lines on Cubic Surfaces

The 27 lines on the cubic surface and the 28 bitangents to the general quartic plane curve are well-known to be closely related to the Weyl groups of E6 and E7. Classical subconfigurations of lines, such as double-sixes, triple systems or Steiner sets, are easily constructed from certain models of the exceptional Lie algebras.

The 27 nsp-spreads of W(3,3) correspond to the 27 lines on a cubic surface under this dictionary, and the 36 double-sixes in the symplectic geometry correspond to the classical 36 double-sixes of lines on the cubic surface.

### Generalized Quadrangle W(q)

The symplectic generalized quadrangle W(q) consists of the points of PG(3,q) together with the totally isotropic lines with respect to a symplectic polarity, forming a GQ of order (q,q). W(q) is isomorphic to the dual of Q(4,q).

### Root System Connection

The five (possibly degenerate) generalized quadrangles where each line has three points correspond to the five root systems in the ADE classes Aₙ, Dₙ, E₆, E₇ and E₈, i.e., the simply laced root systems.

### Summary

The deep connection here is that the automorphism group of the generalized quadrangle W(3,3) is essentially PSp(4,3), and this group is the unique simple group of order 25920 that sits as an index-2 subgroup of the Weyl group W(E6) of order 51840. The 27 lines on a cubic surface (acted on by W(E6)) correspond to 27 nsp-spreads in W(3,3), while the 36 double-sixes of the cubic surface correspond to 36 double-sixes in the symplectic geometry over F₃. This provides a beautiful bridge between finite incidence geometry (generalized quadrangles), algebraic geometry (cubic surfaces), and Lie theory (the E6 root system).
