# Pocket transport glue & orbit 480 summary

This archive contains the concrete outcomes of the two critical counts in the
Wilmot 2026 paper:

1. **Glue of pocket completions.**  There are 540 seven–pockets built from the
   36‑vertex SRG.  When two pockets intersect in four or six vertices we
   consider them adjacent in the overlap graph.  The graph has 2160 edges with
   intersection size four and 270 edges of size six; it is connected.  When
   assigning a global ± sign to each pocket completion, consistency across the
   graph forces all signs equal, yielding exactly 2 solutions (overall
   positive or negative).  Thus the discrete pocket network does *not* admit 480
   distinct glued sign assignments — the 480 multiplicity must come from a
   different symmetry.

2. **Orbit‑480 phenomenon.**  The group of signed permutations of the seven
   imaginary octonion units has order $2^7\,7!=645120$.  The stabilizer of the
   standard multiplication table is size 1344, so the orbit size is
   $645120/1344=480$.  This is the count quoted by Wilmot and observed in our
   earlier computations of octonion orientations.

Together these results show that the 480 appears not as a global glue of
pocket choices, but as the orbit of a single pocket under the full signed
permutation symmetry.
