## Structural Pieces Worth Reviving

### 1. The `360 -> 90 -> 45` Quotient Pattern

The archive repeatedly uses:

- `360` four-center triads in `W33`
- `90` center-quads / K4 components
- a `45`-node quotient `Q`

Bundle `v14` states explicitly that the `90` center-quads form a `2`-cover of
the `45`-point quotient `Q`, with a canonical `Z2` voltage/gauge on quotient
edges. Bundle `v16` identifies the quotient points as `GQ(4,2)` points.

This is promising because `45` already appears in the live program through the
`E6` tritangent count, so this may be an exact quotient geometry rather than
just an embedding convenience.

The current repo already contains the reconstructed incidence data in
`bundles/v13_GQ42_reconstruction/center_quad_gq42_v13/gq42_incidence_check.json`,
with exact counts:

- `45` points
- `27` lines
- `5` points per line
- `3` lines per point
- `135` incidences

So the quotient is not just a vague `45`-node graph. It is the dual
`GQ(4,2)` incidence package, which sits directly on top of the old
`27` / `45` exceptional-count layer already present in the live theory.

### 2. Canonical `Z2` Gauge on the Quotient

Bundle `v14` fixes a canonical gauge by BFS tree normalization and records:

- `5280` quotient triangles total
- `3120` parity-0 triangles
- `2160` parity-1 triangles
- parity-1 fraction `0.4090909090909091 = 9/22`

That is not obviously random. It suggests a genuine `Z2` cocycle / voltage
structure on the quotient, with nontrivial triangle holonomy.

### 3. Universal `-1` Commutator on Directed K4 Moves

Bundle `v7` claims that every directed move within a K4 component has
commutator phase `6 mod 12`, i.e. complex phase `-1`.

If that survives re-verification, it is a strong exact local statement and
looks much more valuable than the embedding scores. It would amount to a
canonical local sign rule on the four-center transport graph.

### 4. Port-Law Compression to Local Data

The `v7` rewrite compresses the transport rule to local data:

- `delta_from_nodes`
- `rem_idx`
- `add_idx`

with the reduced law reproducing the same minimal-cost witnesses as the older
holonomy solver. That means the old phase-aware transport may have an exact
local presentation rather than depending on ad hoc global search state.

### 5. Global Cocycle Constraint

Bundle `v15` adds a global hard constraint:

- cycle K4 component must lie on canonical sheet `0` over its quotient point

This is the first point where the archive clearly distinguishes:

- local transition law
- global `Z2` obstruction / lift condition

That split is mathematically cleaner than the earlier “best embedding”
language.

### 6. Irreducible `S3` Connection

Bundle `v16` is the strongest conceptual step in the archive:

- the canonical `Z2` voltage along the relevant quotient path is `0`
- but the required port transport is an odd `S3` permutation
  `perm = (0,2,1)`

So the transport is not reducible to `Z2`; it needs a genuine non-abelian
`S3` connection on the fiber of local port frames.

This is the most important old idea recovered from the archive.

The current repo also already contains related exact transport work:

- `tests/test_transport_law.py` verifies a `270`-edge transport law.
- `tests/test_s3_sheet_transport.py` verifies the exact `S3` / `C3` sheet
  transport package on `54` pockets and `270` transport edges.

So part of the old N12_58 program was already absorbed into theorem-style code;
it just never got connected cleanly to the newer bridge narrative.
